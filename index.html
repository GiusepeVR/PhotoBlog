The idea behind photos

Images with mysterious elements make people active viewers. No matter what device or lens you employ, solving a puzzle is a big part of its appeal to most professional photographers. The audience will feel a sense of accomplishment and appreciation when they discover the hidden message in your image. Crafting an emotional mind trip for your audience can be a rewarding exercise for both you and them.

“Seeing is the fastest action a human can perform, but at the same time, we only see a single object.” Leonardo da Vinci, A Treatise on Painting

Controlling the direction of the gaze

Framing

Framing emphasizes the importance of the objects that are placed within it. The framing technique can be useful when trying to get an original shot of something well known. The Eiffel Tower, for example, can look much more interesting when photographed through a window. Arches, shutters, doors and holes can serve as a frame. If the image is especially striking, you can use a thinner frame. On the other hand, if it's just an image of a blue sky, thicker frames can stand on their own.

Manage Emotions

The emotional background is created unconsciously. If you analyze the content, the dominant color exerts a psychological influence that we cannot control. The person behind the camera can create a specific mood by selecting colors.

Red

It's the color of desire and passion. Red brings with it energy, life force, and danger at the same time.

Riga Express, Moscow

Yellow

It's a color that improves mood and increases appetite. Anything yellow attracts the viewer with a smile that conveys that life is wonderful.

Glass Distortion, Izhevsk

Green

It's the color of harmony and growth. Light green is a perfect color for children playing in nature, creating the impression of growing in a safe environment. It's no surprise that the signal to move forward is a green light.

Morning Practice, Moscow

Blue

It's the color of safety and trust. Unlike yellow, blue distances the viewer, creating a slight sense of sadness.

Airport, Izhevsk

Purple

This color creates an impression of dignity, grandeur (especially if it has scarlet hues), self-sufficiency, and creativity.

Sunset Port, Novorossiysk

White

White can mean many different things depending on the culture, but it generally represents goodness, light, and life, including life beyond death.

Winter River, Veretyevo

Black

Black is usually associated with ideas of evil and death, though as a clothing color and for electronics, it often conveys a sense of order, elegance, and mysterious power.

Embankment, Moscow

Sharing!

Photos from mobile devices double the fun - one great original shot followed by endless conversations about it on social media.

“Painting with other artists is better than painting alone - healthy envy pushes you to want to be among the most acclaimed, and praise from others is always encouraging.”” Leonardo da Vinci, A Treatise on Painting

Don't forget to share your work, as this way the number of “Likes” and questions you'll get will tell you what you're doing right. And when you get enough uploads, you can look at them again and discover something you didn't know about yourself. For example, ask yourself what the ideas that capture your attention are about - what do you think about the things you photographed, how has your attitude changed over time, and how have you changed yourself? You may find the answers in your work.
